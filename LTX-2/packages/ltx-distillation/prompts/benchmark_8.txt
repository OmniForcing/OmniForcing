Realistic documentary style. A Japanese blacksmith in his sixties, wearing a soot-stained apron over a dark indigo shirt, raises a short-handled hammer above a glowing orange blade resting on an anvil. The camera frames a tight close-up of the strike — sparks erupt in a bright fan across the dim workshop. Each hammer blow rings out sharp and metallic, echoing off stone walls while the charcoal forge crackles and hisses behind him. Warm amber light from the forge illuminates the sweat on his brow. He tilts the blade, inspecting the edge, and the camera slowly pulls back to reveal rows of finished knives hanging on a wooden rack.
Realistic handheld. Early morning mist clings to a calm lake surface as a lone kayaker glides into frame from the right, her paddle dipping rhythmically. The camera follows from the shore in a slow pan, catching the reflection of pine trees mirrored in the still water. Each paddle stroke produces a gentle splash and drip, blending with the distant call of a loon echoing across the valley. Soft pink dawn light bathes the scene. The kayaker pauses, resting the paddle across her lap, and the camera holds on the expanding ripples as they reach the pebbled shore.
Realistic long-lens telephoto. A cheetah crouches low in golden savanna grass, its spotted coat nearly invisible against the dry landscape. The camera captures a compressed telephoto shot, heat shimmer distorting the background acacia trees. The wind whispers through the grass, and a distant zebra herd stamps nervously, hooves thudding on packed earth. Late afternoon sun hits the cheetah's amber eyes at a sharp angle. It tenses, shifts weight to its front paws, then explodes forward in a blur of spots — the camera pans to follow the burst of acceleration as dust kicks up behind it.
Realistic slow dolly back. An old vinyl record player sits on a cluttered desk in a dimly lit student apartment, the needle tracing the grooves of a jazz LP. The camera starts in an extreme close-up of the stylus riding the record surface, then slowly pulls back to reveal stacked books, an open laptop, and a steaming mug. The warm crackle of vinyl underlies a mellow saxophone melody that fills the small room. A desk lamp casts a warm amber pool, leaving the corners in soft shadow. The record label spins steadily, and the faintest scratch artifact punctuates the music every few seconds.
Realistic over-the-shoulder. A young female surgeon in green scrubs and a surgical cap stands at a scrub sink, methodically washing her hands under running water. The camera frames her from behind at shoulder height, catching her reflection in the mirror — focused eyes, no expression. She looks up at her own reflection, takes a steadying breath, and says firmly to herself: "You've done this a hundred times. Stay sharp." She turns off the faucet with her elbow, the water cutting to silence, and holds her dripping hands upward as she backs through the OR door, letting it swing shut behind her.
Realistic medium shot. A street food vendor in Bangkok lifts a wok from a roaring gas burner, tossing pad thai high into the air with a practiced flick. The camera captures the toss from across the narrow stall counter, noodles arcing against a backdrop of hanging bare bulbs and passing motorbikes. He catches the food cleanly, plates it onto a banana leaf, and slides it forward, saying with a grin: "Spicy, just like you asked!" The customer laughs and replies: "Last time you said that, I couldn't feel my tongue for a week." Flames lick the edge of the wok, and the sizzle of oil mixes with the honk of tuk-tuks on the street behind.
Realistic dolly-back wide shot. Two elderly men sit across a stone chess table in a Mediterranean plaza at golden hour, one leaning forward to move a bishop. The camera slowly pulls back, revealing the wider square — a fountain, pigeons, a boy on a bicycle. The first man taps the piece down with a decisive click and says with a dry smile: "Check. And this time, don't pretend you didn't see it." The other leans back, arms crossed, studying the board, and mutters: "I wasn't pretending. I was being generous." Warm light stretches long shadows across the flagstones, and the fountain's gentle splash mixes with distant church bells tolling the hour.
Realistic tight two-shot. A bride and groom stand face-to-face under a simple wooden arch in a garden ceremony, soft afternoon light filtering through a canopy of wisteria. The camera frames them from the chest up, shallow depth of field blurring the seated guests behind. The groom swallows hard, voice cracking slightly as he says: "I wrote my own vows, but honestly, looking at you right now, I forgot every word." Quiet laughter ripples from the guests. She reaches up, straightens his tie, and says softly: "Then just say the important part." He smiles, steadies himself, and whispers: "I'm yours." A string quartet plays a delicate ascending phrase in the background.
